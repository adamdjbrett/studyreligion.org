When people find out that you're a religion major, a lot of questions usually follow, but one of the most common is "What are you going to do with that?" The question is entirely reasonable: while the college years should provide an opportunity for open intellectual exploration of all kinds, the awaiting job market is very competitive. A major course of study should play a significant role in helping you get ahead in life.

On this page you will learn about some of the options available to undergrads who decide to focus on religion. Religion majors have a number of pathways open to them; below you will find descriptions of these possibilities, with special attention to:

  • The preparation religious studies offers for a wide variety of jobs and careers.
  • Graduate study and teaching careers (in high school and college)
  • Graduate study and careers in religious professions
    (e.g., the ministry or rabbinate, religious counseling, etc.)

As you'll see, a religion major is a great way to start your career, whatever it may be.
